02 · Introduction

What are
graphs?

A graph is a mathematical structure used to model pairwise relations between objects — consisting of nodes (vertices) connected by edges (links).

G = (V, E) V = vertices E = edges
02 · Why Graph?

Everything is connected

Economic

Geopolitical

Graph theory provides one of the simplest ways of expressing complex data and structure — serving as a universal language across computer science, economics, physics, chemistry, and sociology.

02 · Origins

Where it began

1736 · Königsberg

Leonhard Euler posed the famous Seven Bridges of Königsberg problem: can one walk through the city crossing each of seven bridges exactly once?

His proof that no such path exists founded graph theory and the concept of Eulerian paths.

KEY INSIGHT

Landmasses → nodes
Bridges → edges

05 · Systemic Risk
Research Highlight

Forecasting financial crashes

Random Financial Network

Dots = financial institutions
Links = cross-holdings
Edge thickness = exposure size

Computational Challenge

Even with complete knowledge of all assets and cross-holdings in a network of just 20–30 institutions, classical computation would take longer than the age of the universe to solve.

13.7 billion years NP-hard

Solution: Quantum Computing

Quantum annealing maps the crash-prediction problem to an Ising model, enabling exponential speedup for graph-structured financial networks.

Physical Review A, 2019
05 · Contagion

Economic contagion pathways

Analogous to disease spreading through protein interaction networks, economic shocks propagate through interconnected financial systems.

SNS Rumors

Misinformation cascades through social graphs, triggering panic and market reactions before fact-checking can occur.

Bank Run Dynamics

Depositor networks create coordination failure; one node's withdrawal signals others to follow.

Systemic Risk

Interconnected balance sheets mean one institution's failure cascades across the network.

07 · Applications

Graph-powered recommendation

RippleNet Architecture

Propagates user preferences through a knowledge graph to discover items the user may not have directly interacted with.

Triple structure:

Entity → relation → Entity
  • User watches Cast Away → Tom Hanks
  • Tom Hanks → starred in → Forrest Gump
  • Recommend: Forrest Gump

Related Research

  • KGCN — Knowledge Graph Convolutional Networks for Recommender Systems (WWW 2019)
  • KGNN-LS — Label Smoothness for KG-aware recommendation
  • KGAT — Knowledge Graph Attention Network (KDD 2019)
  • RippleNet — Propagating user preferences on the KG (RecSys 2018)

Financial application

Same graph traversal logic applies to: stock co-movement networks, analyst report knowledge graphs, supply-chain risk recommendation.
